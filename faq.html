Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

General Information

CivicConnect is a platform for citizens to report non-emergency city issues directly to municipal departments.

Any resident or visitor within the city limits can report issues through this portal.

You can report a wide range of issues like potholes, street light outages, garbage collection, and more.

Do not use CivicConnect for emergencies. Please call 911 or your local emergency services immediately for life-threatening situations or crimes in progress.

Repairs are funded through existing municipal budgets and taxpayer dollars allocated for infrastructure and city maintenance.

Currently, we are a web-based platform optimized for mobile browsers, but our dedicated Android and iOS apps are coming in Summer 2026.

Yes, we use industry-standard encryption to protect your information. Your contact details are only shared with the specific department handling your report.

How to Report

Click the "Report Issue" button and follow the step-by-step form.

Yes, you can choose to report issues anonymously. However, providing contact info helps the city follow up for more details.

You can upload JPG or PNG files directly in the report form.

You can manually drag the pin on the map to the exact location or type in a specific landmark or street address in the location field.

Once submitted, a report cannot be edited. However, you can add a "Follow-up Comment" or additional photos by clicking on your report in the "My Issues" tab.

CivicConnect is for public infrastructure only. For issues on private property (like a neighbor's overgrown yard), please contact the Code Enforcement department directly.

Tracking Your Issue

  • Pending: We have received your report.
  • In Progress: City crews are working.
  • Resolved: The issue is addressed.
